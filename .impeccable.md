## Design Context

### Users
Accounts Repo is used across a Malaysia Sdn Bhd year-end review workflow. The interface must balance accounting preparers, reviewer partners, and client directors: preparers need fast review-pack work and correction commits, reviewers need confidence in the FS impact diff, and directors need clear sign-off confidence without accounting-system clutter.

### Brand Personality
Trustworthy, accountant-native, and restrained. The product should feel like financial source control: authoritative enough for immutable audit evidence, familiar enough for repository-style workflows, and simple enough for finance-literate clients to inspect without training.

### Aesthetic Direction
Better Hub inspired, adapted natively rather than copied. Prefer a simplified light interface with strong contrast, fewer cards, less decoration, and a clearer hierarchy around one active review workflow. Remove ornamental surfaces, redundant copy, and competing visual treatments before adding new style.

### Design Principles
- Show the next review decision first; keep secondary evidence accessible but quieter.
- Use repository metaphors only when they clarify accounting custody, diff, approval, or audit trail.
- Flatten the UI: spacing and alignment should do more work than nested cards, shadows, and decorative backgrounds.
- Keep language plain, short, and action-oriented for both accountants and directors.
- Preserve auditability and accessibility while reducing visible complexity.
